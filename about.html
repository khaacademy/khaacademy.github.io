What is Kha Academy?

Kha Academy is a collection of Mu Alpha Theta practice tests and resources that you can study from! It was made for the future generations at Haynes Academy. Haynes is well-known for their sweeping at local tournaments, and I would like to help Haynes continue this tradition.
Note that this website is mainly made for the Mu students (those who are doing Calculus in Mu Alpha Theta).

What are the Mu, Alpha, Theta levels?

Theta covers Geometry and Algebra II. Alpha covers Pre-Calculus. Mu covers Calculus. My resources are primarily for Mu students (as my only contest experience is in the Mu level, so the only tests I have are from Mu tests).
Some divisions can have specific branches (such as Trigonometry for Alpha) and there can even be middle school divisions. Potpourri is a category consisting of "fun" math.

What happens at a Mu Alpha Theta tournament?

They are typically structured like this: Welcome -> Individual Round -> Team Round -> Interschool Round -> Award Ceremony.
During the welcome, there is a cheesy opening speech and you are usually given the schedule for the day along with a map.
During the individual round, you work on a math test by yourself.
During the team round, you work on a math test with a team from the same school.
During the interschool round, you work on a math test with your whole school. These question cover random topics.
At award ceremony, you see who wins. There are usually trophies for top three and ribbons for honorable mentions (which are like close fourth and fifth).
Sometimes, team or interschool rounds can be substituted for other minigames, such as ciphering, math bowl, gemini, descartes, and hustle. (See more details at http://www.lamao.org/Public/Rules?EventGroup=Games).
